Practical activities

The practical activities are aimed towards building confidence with specific programming sequences in R.

In week 7 and 8 you will be working on more complex programming functions through the practical exercises. These activities will feed into the completion of the problem solving assessment items.

Week 7 and 8 practical activities

The Practical activities (go to Resources>Workshops/Practicals)  provide opportunity to apply what has been covered in the learning materials.

Remember, the tasks have been designed to be tackled independently after the theoretical content is covered.

The practical content contains descriptions of scenarios that are similar to the problems presented in weeks 7 and 8. The intention is for you to adapt the existing models as needed to these practical problems.

We’ll start with some simple examples similar to the Toy Company problem, then methodically move into the harder models.

Have you worked ahead?

Depending on how familiar you are with the R environment, you may have worked ahead.

It might be worth revisiting these pracs. once you have engaged with the learning materials for weeks 7 and 8 to ensure that you understood these materials correctly.
