Example - Assembly line

Pre-modelling Calculations

Sometimes a bit of number crunching is required in order to obtain the correct values for our model. Since our objective and constraints are time-based and dependent on the number of soldiers and trains produced, we’ll want to accumulate some of these values together.

The maximum number of minutes available for each of the workstations are:

Formulating the Model

Decision Variables:

Let s{"version":"1.1","math":"\(s\)"} be the number of soldiers to be produced and t{"version":"1.1","math":"\(t\)"} 

be the number of trains to be produced.

Objective Function:

Since we want to minimise the amount of idle time of the workstations, it follows that we want to subtract our production times from the total amount of available time. Setting this up as a worded equation gives us:

minimise (Idle Time) = (Total Time) - (Total Time Spent Producing Soldiers) - (Total Time Spent Producing Trains)

which translates into the following minimisation objective function:

min z=1470−12s−19t{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ \textrm{min } z = 1470-12s-19t \\"}

Constraints: The only constraints we have pertains to each workstation, so for Workstations 1, 2, and 3 respectively we have the following constraints:

3s+6t≤540{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ 3s+6t \leq 540 \\"}

5s+5t≤450{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ 5s+5t \leq 450 \\"}

4s+8t≤480{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ 4s+8t \leq 480 \\"}

Along with our non-negativity constraints (i.e. the number of soldiers and trains produced cannot be a negative number), our full LP model is:
