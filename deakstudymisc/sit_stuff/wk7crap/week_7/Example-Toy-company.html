Example - Toy company

We are in charge of a toy company currently producing two types of toys: soldiers and trains.

After some research and data collection/analysis, these are the facts that we have determined:

Each soldier built:

  • sells for $27{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\$27\)"} and uses $10{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\$10\)"} worth of raw materials
  • costs x$14$$ for labour per unit
  • requires 2 hours of finishing labour per unit
  • requires 1 hour of carpentry labour per unit

Each train built: 

  • sells for $21{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\$21\)"} and uses $9{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\$9\)"} worth of raw materials
  • costs $10{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\$10\)"} for labour per unitrequires 1 hour of finishing labour per unit
  • requires 1 hour of carpentry labour per unit

Resources:

  • unlimited raw material
  • 100 hours of available finishing labour every week
  • 80 hours of available carpentry labour every week

Demand:

  • since trains are a new product, demand is unknown
  • previous data shows that at least 40 soldiers are bought each week 

We’ll make the assumption that every product produced is sold, and that our objective is to maximise our profit. Before we build an LP model, though, it may help to organise the above data into a table and do some calculations.

Decision Variables

Let s{"version":"1.1","math":"\(s\)"} be the number of soldiers to be produced each week, and t{"version":"1.1","math":"\(t\)"} be the number of trains to be produced each week.

Constraints

Each week, we cannot use more than 100 hours of finishing labour. We can read this off the first column of our table.

2s+t≤100{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ 2s+t \leq 100 \\"}

Each week, we cannot use more than 80 hours of carpentry labour, which reads off the second column of our table.

s+t≤80{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ s+t \leq 80 \\"}

Maximum weekly demand of 40 soldiers.

s≥40{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ s \geq 40 \\"}

Non-negativity constraints (since we cannot have a negative amount of soldiers and trains).

s,t≥0{"version":"1.1","math":"\\ s, t \geq 0 \\"}
