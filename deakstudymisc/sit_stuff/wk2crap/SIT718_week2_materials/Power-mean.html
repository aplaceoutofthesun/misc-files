Power Mean

The power mean is best seen as a generalised mean which encompasses the arithmetic, geometric and harmonic means. In other words, we can obtain the arithmetic/geometric/harmonic mean from the power mean by specifying a specific p{"version":"1.1","math":"\(p\)"} value.

The power mean for an input vector x=(x1,x2,…,xn){"version":"1.1","math":"\(\mathbf{x} = (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n)\)"} with xi≥0{"version":"1.1","math":"\(x_i \geq 0\)"} for all i{"version":"1.1","math":"\(i\)"} is:

PMp(x)=(1n∑i=1nx1p))1p=(x1p+x2p+⋯+xnpn)1p{"version":"1.1","math":"PM_p(\mathbf{x}) = \left( \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n x_1^p) \right)^{\frac{1}{p}} = \left( \frac{ x_1^p + x_2^p + \cdots + x_n^p }{n} \right)^{\frac{1}{p}} "}

Recall: a value to a fractional power 1p{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\frac{1}{p}\)"} is the same as taking the p-th root. e.g. x13=x3{"version":"1.1","math":"\(x^\frac{1}{3} = \sqrt[3]{x}\)"}

Substituting p=1{"version":"1.1","math":"\(p=1\)"} and p=−1{"version":"1.1","math":"\(p=-1\)"} into the power mean formula will obtain the formula for the arithmetic mean and harmonic mean respectively, but not so much for p=0{"version":"1.1","math":"\(p=0\)"} and the geometric mean as we cannot have the fractional power 10{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\frac{1}{0}\)"}. It can be mathematically proven that if we approach p=0{"version":"1.1","math":"\(p=0\)"} then the result will approach the geometric mean, but this is outside of the focus of this program.
