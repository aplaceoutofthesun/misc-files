Assessments

There are two different types of assessments: quizzes and problem solving tasks.

Completing the bi-weekly quizzes provides a check point that will give you a sense of how you are progressing in the unit. The problem-solving assessments will require a response to case studies and real world scenarios. This will require higher order thinking.

Assessment task 1: Quizzes

Quizzes involve an assortment of multiple-choice and true/false questions that aim to provide quick feedback on your understanding of the material.

Assessment tasks 2, 3 and 4:

Assessment 2 and 3 are problem-solving tasks, Assessment 4 is 2 hours examination.

Problem solving tasks are written assessments with a number of questions that involve applying what you’ve learnt to a data set and/or described scenario. They are designed to measure your ability to apply the techniques in this program to scenarios, and to correctly interpret the results. The following outlines will give you a brief introduction to these tasks. However, it is important that you familiarise yourself with the details of all the assignments by viewing the full assignment brief documents.
